📖 CNGM SYSTEM MANUAL

1. MANIFESTO & PHILOSOPHY

YOUR LIBRARY. MANAGEABLE. SEARCHABLE. PORTABLE.

I wanted an app that could organize and launch all my games, from Steam, Heroic (Epic and GOG), Flatpak, Emulators or whatever in just one place.

I wanted it to be complete with all the features I've always wished I had in a Linux game manager, like checking Youtube videos, HowLongToBeat, and ProtonDB right from inside the app, among other things.

I wanted it to be simple, beautiful and themeable.

And most importantly, I wanted it to be completely PORTABLE and BACKUPABLE.

THE LOCAL-ONLY PHILOSOPHY

CNGM is built with a strict Offline-First, Local-Only philosophy for your media. Once you fetch artwork, covers, logos, or download a trailer, it is saved securely into your local GameManagerConfig folder.

This means your library will never suffer from "link rot" or broken web images if a storefront changes its API. It also means you can pack up the entire folder, put it on a thumb drive, and take your flawlessly curated library to another PC entirely.

THE LAUNCH COMMAND

The crucial feature for my own usage. Inside the Detailed View, there is a field where you can put a command to launch that game.

Anything goes. If you can launch it on Linux, you can launch it from here. You can even open your calculator or your terminal from here if that fancies your desire.

Now the sweet part: If you imported your games via Steam or Heroic sync, the launch commands will be filled out automatically, even if the game is not installed (clicking PLAY will prompt you to install it).

2. ADDING GAMES & STORE SYNC

Before we dive in, a quick note: all data is securely stored in a single folder created in the same directory as the app (`GameManagerConfig`). No searching around your entire OS in hidden folders.

CONNECTING YOUR STORES

Click the Connect button in the left Menu sector to begin syncing libraries.

  • HEROIC SYNC (EPIC/GOG/AMAZON): Automatically scans your local Heroic/Legendary config files and pulls in everything you own (installed or not) with the correct native launch commands! This includes your Amazon Games library if you have it connected in Heroic.
  • STEAM: Input your SteamID64 and API key. The system grabs your entire library and injects the launch commands automatically. (Your profile's 'Game Details' must be set to Public.)
  • GOG: Opens a secure internal login window. Once logged in, close it, and the app will automatically scrape and pull in your owned GOG assets. The best way to import GOG games is actually via Heroic Sync, but if you want you can use this GOG Sync (be aware that you will have to add ALL launch commands manually).

ADDING GAMES MANUALLY

Don't want to sync? Hit the + Add Game button. It creates a blank slate. From there, you can jump to the Edit Details screen, type the title, and hit ✨ Auto-Fetch to pull all metadata and artwork from the Steam database instantly.

3. VIEWS & NAVIGATION

There are three main views. The left sidebar remains your constant command center for filtering and searching.

THE LIST VIEW

For the true data obsessive. A high-density spreadsheet showing you everything at a glance: Launch status, Favs, Want, Store, Genre, and Release Year. Double click a row to open the Gamepage. If Gaming History is enabled, your most recently played games will be pinned at the very top.

THE GALLERY VIEW

All about the cover art. A highly visual, grid-based layout. At the top, the dynamic Hero header acts as a screensaver for your currently filtered selection, utilizing a beautiful Ken Burns effect to fade smoothly between the hero arts and screenshots of the games you are currently viewing. Just like the List View, your recent games will appear first if the history tracking is active.

SIDEBAR FILTERS

The See section of the sidebar lets you instantly narrow your library by store or category: All Games, Playable, Favs, Want, Steam, Epic, GOG, Amazon Games, Apps, Physical, Others, and Emulation.

One quality-of-life detail worth knowing: if you're deep inside a Gamepage or the Edit Details screen and you click a filter button, CNGM will automatically navigate you back to the last grid view you were using (List or Gallery) and apply the filter. No need to manually backtrack first.

THE CREMA COMPANION

If CREMA.AppImage is present in the same folder as CNGM, a Go Fullscreen With / CREMA button will appear at the bottom of the sidebar. Clicking it minimizes CNGM and launches CREMA — the fullscreen, gamepad-driven companion app — in its own process. Both apps share the same library database seamlessly.

4. THE PERFECT GAMEPAGE

Double-click any entry to enter the immersive Gamepage. This is your premium storefront view for the game.

STEP-BY-STEP: CRAFTING THE ULTIMATE PAGE

To get the absolute best-looking, most complete Gamepage, follow this workflow:

  • 1. The Initial Scrape: Click ⚙️ EDIT DETAILS and click ✨ AUTO-FETCH. This pulls the Cover, Hero Art, Logo, Icon, up to 5 Screenshots, the massive HTML description, and queries ProtonDB and HLTB automatically.
  • 2. Refine the Visuals: Still in the Edit screen, look at the left column. If the Steam-provided Hero Art or Logo looks boring, click the SGDB button. This connects to SteamGridDB (requires a free API key) to let you pick custom, fan-made transparent logos and gorgeous textless hero backgrounds.
  • 3. Add the Trailer: Click ▶ WATCH TRAILER. It will search YouTube for a high-quality, no-commentary gameplay trailer. Select the best one to download it to your local drive.
  • 4. Enjoy the Results: Return to the Gamepage. You should now see:
    • A massive Hero banner with a crisp, centered transparent Logo.
    • An animated Screenshots banner utilizing the Ken Burns effect. Clicking it opens a fullscreen slideshow!
    • A beautifully formatted short description summarizing the game, followed by the rich HTML Steam storefront payload.
    • A 🎬 WATCH TRAILER button right in the action bar, ready to play your locally downloaded video instantly.

From the Gamepage, you can also use the live toggle buttons (+ FAV and WANT TO PLAY) to instantly update the database without needing to open the Edit screen.

The stats panel also displays Similar Games if you've added them — a handy personal recommendation field for games that share the same vibe.

5. DETAILED VIEW & FALLBACKS

Click ⚙️ EDIT DETAILS to enter Edit Mode. Here you have full control over the database entry. You can edit every single text box manually.

HLTB & PROTONDB FALLBACKS

Next to the HLTB (HowLongToBeat) and ProtonDB fields, you'll see a magnifying glass icon (🔍). Clicking this queries the live HowLongToBeat and ProtonDB APIs and drops the data straight into your text box. HLTB in particular uses a direct integration against their current API, so the numbers you get are real — not guesses from a stale package.

However, if the automated query fails (the game title is highly unusual, or the API is momentarily grumpy), the app will intelligently pop open a secure internal web browser window directly to the search results page. You can read the score or time, close the window, and type it in manually. No dead ends.

6. MEDIA, SGDB & TRAILERS

STEAMGRIDDB (SGDB)

By pasting a free SteamGridDB API key into the app's settings, clicking the SGDB button next to any asset will instantly load the top-rated custom artwork for your game. If the auto-search gets the wrong game (e.g., pulling art for "Doom 2016" instead of "Doom 1993"), use the manual search box located inside the SGDB window to hunt for specific variations or editions. Click an image, and it downloads and applies it automatically.

YOUTUBE TRAILERS & THE LOCAL RULE

Clicking ▶ WATCH TRAILER in the edit screen queries YouTube. Select the best result, and the app uses an embedded yt-dlp backend to securely process and download the highest-quality MP4 to your local folder.

IMPORTANT: The 🎬 WATCH TRAILER button on the main Gamepage will only appear if the game has a trailer successfully downloaded to your local drive. CNGM will never attempt to stream videos directly from the internet on the Gamepage. This guarantees that if the button is there, the video will play instantly, at maximum quality, entirely offline.

7. TOOLS, DATA & CLEANUP

Click the Tools button in the left sidebar to open the Control Center. It is split into two columns: Preferences on the left, Actions on the right.

APPEARANCE

The top card on the left combines two visual preferences in one place. Theme Selection opens the theme picker — organized by category — where you can live-preview any colour scheme without committing. Below it, the UI Scale segmented control lets you zoom the entire interface between 50% and 150%, which is particularly useful on Steam Deck or high-DPI displays.

GAMING HISTORY

Set how many recently launched games CNGM should pin to the top of both views (OFF, 5, 10, 15, or 20). Use Clear History to wipe the timestamps without touching anything else in the database.

LIBRARY

The powerhouse card on the right. Batch Fetch Missing Artwork scans your entire library in the background — it checks all five asset types (Cover, Hero, Logo, Icon, Screenshots) and silently pulls whatever is missing from Steam. Run it once after a big sync and let it do the heavy lifting.

Below that: Import CSV and Export CSV let you move your library in and out of a standard spreadsheet format, and Template downloads a blank CSV with all the correct column headers so you can bulk-add games the analogue way.

SYSTEM

Backup ZIP packs your entire GameManagerConfig folder — database, covers, logos, screenshots, everything — into a single archive. Restore ZIP unpacks it. When you're moving to a new machine or a Steam Deck, this is the only thing you need.

Add to Application Menu registers both CNGM and CREMA as entries in your desktop environment's application launcher (GNOME, KDE, XFCE, etc.). It writes the .desktop files, extracts the icons, and refreshes the launcher database automatically — no terminal required. Run it again any time you move the folder to update the paths.

CLEANUP

Because of the neurotic local-only philosophy, and the fact that SGDB custom artwork generates unique filenames, your images folder quietly accumulates orphaned files whenever you swap covers or logos.

  • Clean Images: Cross-references every image currently assigned in the database and deletes anything that is no longer attached to a game. Fast, safe, surgical.
  • Clear All Images: The nuclear option. Wipes all downloaded artwork and screenshots from disk and resets the visual database entirely. Use only if you want a completely fresh start.
  • Wipe Secure Browser Data: Clears the internal browser cache used for the GOG login window and any Steam API popups. If something is behaving strangely after a store sync, try this first.
